About Me

I earned my Ph.D. in Environmental Science from University of California, Merced at Water Systems Management lab under the mentorship of Professor Josue Medellin-Azuara. My doctoral research was on conserving birds on California’s farmlands using dynamic flooded habitats.

Currently, I study climate change and land use impacts on coastal bird species at the National Center of Ecological Analysis and Sythesis (NCEAS). I use machine learning models and citizen science Ebird data to delineate the variations in migratory species population across time and space in the Gulf of Mexico.

I am working on how to use state of art artificial intelligences to handle the multi-modality big data better in generating information for the Gulf’s restoration decision-making support
